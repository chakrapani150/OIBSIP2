Sardar Vallabhbhai Patel

Iron Man of India

A brief summary of Sardar Vallabhbhai Patel journey

Early Life

Vallabhbhai Patel was born on October 31, 1875 in Nadiad village of modern day Gujarat to Zaverbai and Ladbai. Education

Starting his academic career in a Gujarati medium school, Sardar Vallabhbhai Patel later shifted to an English medium school. In 1897, Vallabhbhai passed his high school and started preparing for law examination. He went to pursue a degree in law and travelled to England in 1910. He completed his law degree in 1913 from Inns of Court and came back to India to start his law practice in Godhra, Gujarat. For his legal proficiency, Vallabhbhai was offered many lucrative posts by the British Government but he rejected all. He was a staunch opponent of the British government and its laws and therefore decided not to work for the British.

Legal Career

Sardar Vallabhbhai Patel's successful legal career demonstrated his commitment to justice, fairness, and the rights of the people. His legal expertise and leadership skills played a significant role in shaping the course of India's independence movement and its legal framework post-independence.

Political Journey

Sardar Vallabhbhai Patel's active participation in the Indian National Congress and various freedom movements showcased his unwavering commitment to the cause of Indian independence. His leadership, resilience, and strategic approach were instrumental in shaping the course of the freedom struggle and laying the foundation for a united and independent India.

Role in Indian Independence

Patel's negotiation skills were crucial during critical moments of the freedom struggle. He played a key role in negotiations with the British, ensuring that the demands of the Indian National Congress and the Indian people were effectively conveyed and represented. Even after independence, Patel continued to contribute significantly to the nation-building process. He served as the first Deputy Prime Minister and Home Minister of India, overseeing crucial responsibilities such as unification, administrative reforms, and maintaining internal security.

Integration of Princely States

Patel's most significant achievement was the integration of over 500 princely states into the newly independent India. His vision and determined efforts ensured that these states joined the Indian Union, thereby consolidating the territorial integrity and unity of India.

Legacy

Patel's efforts in uniting a diverse and fragmented nation have left a lasting legacy of national unity. Today, India stands as a vibrant and diverse democracy, where people from different cultures, religions, and backgrounds coexist as one nation. Patel's vision of a united India serves as a reminder of the strength that lies in unity.
